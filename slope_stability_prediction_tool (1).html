What is the Factor of Safety (FoS)?

The Factor of Safety is the ratio of the resisting forces (shear strength) to the driving forces (shear stress) acting on a slope. A FoS ≥ 1.5 is generally considered stable for most engineering applications. Values below 1.0 indicate that failure has already occurred or is imminent.

This tool uses a trained XGBoost machine learning model to predict FoS from five key geotechnical parameters, providing rapid assessment without requiring full limit equilibrium analysis.

Input Parameters

Slope Height H (m): Vertical height of the slope from toe to crest. Taller slopes exert greater gravitational force and reduce stability.

Slope Angle β (°): Angle of the slope face measured from horizontal. Steeper slopes are generally less stable.

Cohesion c (kPa): Inherent shear strength of the soil independent of normal stress. Higher cohesion improves stability.

Friction Angle φ (°): Angle of internal friction of the soil. Higher values indicate greater shear resistance.

Unit Weight γ (kN/m³): Weight of soil per unit volume. Affects the magnitude of driving and resisting forces.

Groundwater Condition: Presence and level of water table. Groundwater increases pore pressure, reducing effective stress and stability.

Model Information

The prediction engine uses XGBoost (Extreme Gradient Boosting) with the following configuration: 130 estimators, max depth 2, learning rate 0.05, L1 regularization (α=0.1). The model was validated using 5-fold cross-validation and tested on a held-out dataset.

The sensitivity analysis feature varies one parameter across its physical range while holding all others constant at your entered values — this reveals which parameters most significantly influence the predicted FoS for your specific slope geometry.
